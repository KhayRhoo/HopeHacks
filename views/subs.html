Consulting with health provider about vaccination

It will take several weeks for the vaccine to become fully active after you receive it. Do not wait for cases of COVID-19 to spike before getting vaccinated. You will be better protected if you get vaccinated in advance of the next wave of infections. If you have already been vaccinated, keep track of how long it has been since you completed the primary vaccination series. If it has been five or more months since you completed the primary vaccination series, consider consulting with your health care provider about getting a booster shot.

Limiting activities to reduce risk of exposure

Ensure taking preventative and cautious measures

Always wash hands and ensure to disenfect commonly used surfaces throughout your home or workplace. Consider having food and grocery items delivered to your home. Create an emergency response plan that you can reply on in case you or a close one is diagnosed with COVID-19.

Treatments

Currently, there is no cure for COVID-19. However, some strategies may be employed to reduce disease severity. The U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommend staying home, drinking plenty of fluids, and isolating yourself away from other people in your home to prevent spreading the coronavirus to them.
